<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Non-invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Animal patient monitors can be categorized into two main types: invasive and non-invasive monitors. Invasive monitors involve the insertion of probes or sensors into the animal's body to directly measure parameters such as blood pressure, temperature, and oxygen levels. Non-invasive monitors, on the other hand, do not require any penetration of the body and instead use external sensors to measure vital signs. Both types play crucial roles in providing accurate and timely health information for veterinarians to monitor and treat animals effectively.</p></p>
