<p><p>Controlled-release drug delivery technology encompasses various types such as the Wurster Technique, Coacervation, Micro Encapsulation, Implants, Transdermal, Targeted Delivery, and others. The Wurster Technique involves coating particles with a polymer to control drug release. Coacervation is a process where drug molecules are encapsulated in a polymer shell. Micro Encapsulation involves enclosing drug molecules in microspheres for slow release. Implants are devices inserted into the body to release drugs over time. Transdermal delivery involves administering drugs through the skin. Targeted Delivery delivers drugs specifically to the desired site in the body. Others refer to additional methods used for controlled drug release.</p></p>
