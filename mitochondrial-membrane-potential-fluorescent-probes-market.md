Inorganic probes, on the other hand, are often metal-containing complexes that exhibit fluorescence when bound to mitochondria. Both types of probes are widely used in research to study mitochondrial function and dynamics, but each type offers unique advantages and limitations depending on the specific research applications.</p></p>
