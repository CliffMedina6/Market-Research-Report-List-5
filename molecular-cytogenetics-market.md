<p><p>Molecular cytogenetics refers to the study of chromosomes at a molecular level. There are several market types within this field including non-radioactive in situ hybridization, comparative genomic hybridization, and in situ hybridization. Non-radioactive in situ hybridization is a technique used to visualize and map specific DNA sequences within cells. Comparative genomic hybridization is a method for analyzing DNA copy number variations in different cell samples. In situ hybridization is a process that helps locate and visualize specific DNA or RNA sequences within cells.</p></p>
