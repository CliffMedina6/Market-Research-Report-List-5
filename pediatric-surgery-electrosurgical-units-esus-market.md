<p><p>Pediatric Surgery Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are medical devices used in pediatric surgeries to cut, coagulate, and seal tissues using electrical energy. There are three main types of ESUs in the market: Monopole ESUs, Bipolar ESUs, and Vessel Sealing Units. Monopole ESUs are used for cutting and coagulation in surgeries. Bipolar ESUs are used for delicate procedures where precise control is needed. Vessel Sealing Units are specifically designed to seal blood vessels during surgeries, reducing bleeding and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
