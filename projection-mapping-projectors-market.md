<p><p>Projection mapping projectors come in two main types: DLP (Digital Light Processing) and LCD (Liquid Crystal Display). DLP projectors use tiny mirrors to reflect light and create an image, resulting in high color contrast and sharpness. LCD projectors, on the other hand, use liquid crystal panels to display images through light polarization. DLP projectors are known for their vibrant colors and fast response times, making them popular for gaming and home theaters. LCD projectors typically offer better brightness and connectivity options, making them ideal for business presentations and classrooms.</p></p>
