<p><p>Sulfate Resisting Cement is classified into two main types based on their sulfate resistance levels: High Sulfate Resisting Cement and Moderate Sulfate Resisting Cement. High Sulfate Resisting Cement is specifically designed to withstand high levels of sulfate exposure, making it ideal for use in areas with severe sulfate attack. Moderate Sulfate Resisting Cement offers a lower level of sulfate resistance compared to the high-grade option but still provides adequate protection in moderate sulfate environments. Both types are crucial for ensuring the durability and longevity of concrete structures in sulfate-rich surroundings.</p></p>
